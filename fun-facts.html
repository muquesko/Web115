What is Autism

First off Autism is a trait. People can tell you it’s where people struggle with social skills, understanding verbal and visual cues, they rock, have sensitivity to any of their five senses, but to us it’s more than that. Can someone tell you in one or two words what is Japan? “Yeah, people are smart there.” Correct answer: no you can’t and Autism is the same way. Believe it or not some of us have fantastic social skills and can give speeches.

History of Autism

1908: Eugen Bleuler was the first to use this term after studying patients with schizophrenia.

1943- Leo Kanner publishes a report describing 11 children who were sheltered and in their own world borrowing the term Autistic to describe them. Leo Kanner was the first to recognize that Autism was not a form of schizophrenia or being mentally handicapped in anyway, but rather to struggle with social norms.

1944-Hans Asperger diagnoses a less severe form of Autism known as Asperger’s, later this creates the Autism spectrum.

1967-Bruno Bettelheim formed a false theory where mothers who were distant or cruel, later this theory were proven false.

1977- Research is being done on two twins finding out that it can be biological.

1980-Autism is no longer compared with schizophrenia and is now listed as a disorder.

1987- Ivar Lovaas publishes an article on how to help children with Autism, with means of behavior therapy, it’s also no longer referred to as “infantile autism” and instead referred to as “Autism disorder”.

1988-Rain Man a movie featuring a man with Autism comes to theatres, however due to this movie a stereotype is built and people in Hollywood start playing all characters with Autism like Rain Man.

1991-People start recognizing Autism on a spectrum and giving service to the kids who have Autism services they need.

1994- Asperger’s starts being recognized as another form of Autism, just lower on the spectrum.

1998-A false claim was made were vaccines cause Autism, Page semi-protected Andrew Wakefield who made this claim had his doctors license taken away.

2000-Though this claim is false people start worrying and thimerosal is removed vaccines.

2009- 1 in 110 people are diagnosed with Autism, later this easement increases.

2013-Autism and Asperger’s are now an umbrella term, were every person with Autism does have some sort of challenges with something social related, like social communication and/or interaction or/and restricted and/or repetitive behaviors. However, every person with Autism is different; again a lot of us love making friends, are public speakers, and have great social skills like anyone else. It’s like any other disability, for example are ALL blind people completely blind? If you answered yes you don’t get out enough, if you answered no then same thing with Autism.

Do's, don'ts, and Autism

Do's

Invite a friend who has Autism to hang out, they have interests and lives like anyone else

Study and learn more about Autism

Join organizations and clubs, learn more about the culture.

Don't

Assume and ask things like "But you are outgoing!?" remember people with Autism CAN be outgoing, there are different kinds, for example being colorblind and being legally blind, both visual impairments, but have different symptoms.

Say inapriopriate things like "But you don't LOOK Autistic?" Autistic isn't a visual characteristic

Make fun of someone or exclude someone who has Autism, remember they want friends just like anyone else.
